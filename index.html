A Nest Report


Welcome to The Master Nest! This is the ultimate resource for Pokémon Go nests. We offer a list of the top worldwide nests organized by Pokémon species, separated by generation; plus a GPX section where you can get a custom route for the nest you wish to play on. Hop around the pages and start hunting!

Our home and base of operations is the CoolDown Club discord server, which you can access [here]. You can find organized raids, rare Pokémon spawns, mini-games and much more! Plus it is the best place to report any discrepancies you may find in this report, as we manually research and submit each nest each migration.

And keep in mind the following list, these are the only species that nest, any other Pokémon is only obtainable through regular spawns, eggs, research or raids!

This is migration 184. This one was very very late!

Hey everyone! It's quite late, a whole migration cycle late in fact! I do want to apologize the inactivity on the site the past few weeks, I just didn't have much time to do research and re-research with all the frameshift migrations and I couldn't keep up at all. I have more time now however, and I am back working on some exciting changes for this site. So definitely keep an eye out for that!

Regarding this migration, it is the first migration after the Season of Light kicked off. And as such, we have a whole new nesting pool, which now features evolved Pokémon, much to my surprise. You can find the entire list of nesting species right above this blog post under the "Show Nesting Species" button, but the most notable new additions to the nesting pool are Weepinbell and Skiploom, Combee, Munna, Stufful, Wimpod and Hisuian Voltorb. Combee, Munna and Stufful can all be shiny, so be sure to look out for them on the Gen IV, Gen V and Gen VII pages respectively. Hisuian Voltorb can be found next to its Kantonian variant on the Gen I page, with a cool custom sprite by VentZX00 on the PokéCommunity forums.

It was really fun to get back to work on our report and research a brand new pool of nesting species. We think we did a good job, but as always you can report a discrepancy over our Discord server [here]. We are human, after all!

Hopefully you find our report useful! We'll see you soon.

Thanks for stopping by; stay healthy and happy hunting!

-Dex
