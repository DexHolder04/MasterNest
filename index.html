This is migration 171. This one was warm and sunny, a trip to an exotic island.

Alola! Okay sorry that was a bit much, hey everyone! Seasons have shifted in Pokémon Go, and that means a lot of changes for nests.

For the Season of Alola, Pokémon originally discovered in the Alola region are making their Pokémon Go debut. We already had Alolan variants available in the game, but this is the first time we've seen original Pokémon from the Sun and Moon games.

Notably for nests, Alolan Geodude and Alolan Rattata are now part of the nesting pool. This is a first in the history of nests in the game, as previously only normal forms of any given Pokémon would be available as a nesting species, never a regional variant. I honestly never thought of that possibility, so I earned myself a few headaches earlier this week thinking of how to implement the variants in the site and in the back-end for our reporters.

We should be good to go now though! A few other things worth noting, Mareep and Petilil are new additions to the nesting pool, handy if you're hunting for Mareep XL Candy.

So here's our report, we think it's pretty spot on but with the current Alola event going on we may have some misreports, so as always please feel free to report any inaccuracies or new findings over our Discord server [here].

Thanks for stopping by; stay healthy and happy hunting!
